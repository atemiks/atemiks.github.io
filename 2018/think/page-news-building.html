Building Your Vision

  • BY ALEX RIDINGS
  • 2 DAYS AGO
  • 3 MINUTE READ
  • SHARE

Building A Bold Vision Can Drive Your Team & Inspire Your Customers

It is not unusual to ask a CEO what his company vision is and be greeted by a should shrug. “I am not sure that’s relevant to us”, “I don’t want to be held to it if our business changes”, “There is so much going on I am not sure I have the focus to come up with something good”, “I just don’t know where to start”, are all common responses. When working with organisations, often in developing their brand, we regularly see concerted uncertainty and frustration from staff and management about where the business they are investing time into is going. So why is it that so many people in strategic roles do not have this seemingly critical component squared in away and acting as their company’s beacon?

Vision statements are generally expected from corporate businesses and organisations. Smaller businesses, often sizeable in turnover and/or scope, largely do not feel it is appropriate or understand their worth and they can lack confidence in adopting them, and this is a mistake. Let us just look at the logic. The premise here is that corporate ideals are not suited to smaller businesses. Yes, corporate refers to large-scale organisations, but part of what sustains their size is often marketing control and confidence in their communications. They have long-term plans, a common organisation wide vision, and work inexorably towards them. Sure, blips and bumps arise, but whilst dealing with those it is their vision and direction that helps drive them forwards.

That same logic should and can apply to smaller businesses and brands. A vision that encapsulates your long-term direction, aspiration and core values can act as the core-marketing framework to your business and plans.

Like corporate businesses, having that common sense of direction understood throughout your business can steer you in the good times and guide you when challenges arise.
Our advice would be to:

  • Create short (50-60 words) vision statement which is directional rather than specifically detailed and which harnesses your ethos, culture and personality.
  • Invest time explaining this to your team, from top to bottom, as it is important everyone is pulling in the same direction
  • Incorporate it in your marketing media upfront and visible.
  • Do not be shy about it. Be bold. Confidence is critical to buy-in from all stakeholders.
